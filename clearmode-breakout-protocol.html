Breaking out right now?

Here's your protocol.

Step away from the mirror. There are exactly three things to do — in this order. No spiraling, no purging your skincare shelf, no new products. Just this.

1

Do This First

Ice it. Twice a day, five minutes at a time.

Wrap an ice cube in a thin cloth and hold it directly on the inflamed area for 5 minutes. Do this morning and evening. Cold vasoconstricts the blood vessels underneath, which reduces redness, swelling, and the inflammatory cascade driving the breakout — without touching your skin barrier or introducing anything new into the equation. It's free. It works. It buys time while everything else catches up.

5 min each session AM + PM Never ice directly on bare skin
2

Play Detective

Write down everything that's changed in the last 72 hours.

Microcomedones take up to 90 days to surface — but an acute flare that appears quickly usually has a recent trigger. Think back across the last three days and write down anything new, different, or off-script. New products, changed products, food, stress, travel, hormonal timing. Patterns don't lie, but you have to record them first.

Dairy Fried foods Peanut butter Shellfish Alcohol Heavy sugar or refined carbs Any new product Skipped steps
3

Spot Treat

If you're not already using a spot treatment, start now.

Applied directly to active breakouts morning and night, a targeted spot treatment gets benzoyl peroxide or salicylic acid exactly where the inflammation is — without over-treating the surrounding skin. These are the two Face Reality products formulated for this exact moment in your protocol.
